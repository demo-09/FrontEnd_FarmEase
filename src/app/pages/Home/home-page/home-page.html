📜 A Rich Legacy

History of Indian Farming

India has been an agricultural civilization for over 8,000 years. Here is how farming evolved to reach the digital era.

~6000 BCE

Indus Valley Agriculture

The Indus Valley Civilization pioneered organized farming — growing wheat, barley, peas, and cotton. They built the world's earliest irrigation canals along the Indus river.

🏺 Ancient Era
~1500–500 BCE

Vedic Agricultural Science

Ancient texts like Rigveda and Arthashastra detailed crop rotation, soil preparation, and advanced storage methods. Farmers were considered the backbone of the Vedic economy.

📜 Vedic Period
300–1700 CE

Mughal & Medieval Agriculture

Mughal emperors introduced Persian wheel irrigation, zamindar land systems, and cash crops like sugarcane and cotton. Agriculture became highly organized and taxed.

🕌 Medieval Era
1960s

Green Revolution

India's historic Green Revolution led by Dr. M.S. Swaminathan transformed agriculture. High-yielding crop varieties, chemical fertilizers, and modern irrigation made India self-sufficient in food production.

🟢 Green Revolution
2000–2020

Digital & Tech-Driven Farming

Internet connectivity, mobile phones, and e-NAM (National Agriculture Market) began transforming how farmers sell their produce. Farmers gained access to market prices via SMS & internet.

📱 Digital Era
2024 → Future

AI + FarmEase Era

FarmEase brings AI-powered recommendations, direct farm-to-buyer marketplaces, smart weather analytics, and real-time inventory management — empowering every Indian farmer.

50% of Earth's Habitable Land is Used for Agriculture

Of Earth's 149 million km² of land, about 51 million km² is used for farming — an area bigger than North and South America combined. Yet over 800 million people still go to sleep hungry every night.
